## Part 1: Neighbor Arbitrage - The Geographic Spillover Effect

### The Concept

**Hypothesis**: Suburbs within the same geographic region share:
- Public transport (same train lines)
- Schools (same catchment areas)
- Beaches/parks (same access)
- Shopping centers
- Hospitals and services
- Social networks

**Yet some suburbs trade at massive discounts to their neighbors.**

### Methodology

1. **Manual Region Assignment**: Classified 1,463 Sydney suburbs into 14 geographic regions based on name patterns
2. **Regional Benchmarking**: Calculated average value score for each region
3. **Arbitrage Identification**: Found suburbs with:
   - Value score < Regional average (underpriced)
   - Quality index > Sydney median (still good quality)
   - Result: 119 opportunities

## Part 2: Volunteer Rate - Community Engagement Analysis

### The Hypothesis

High volunteer rates indicate:
- Engaged parents â†’ better school support
- Social capital â†’ safer communities
- Civic participation â†’ maintained infrastructure
- Stable communities â†’ lower turnover

### Methodology

**Data Source**: 2021 Census Table G23 - "Voluntary work for an organisation or group by age by sex"

**Calculation**:
```
Volunteer_Pct = (Total_Volunteers_15+) / (Total_Persons_15+) Ã— 100
```

**Coverage**: 1,463 Sydney suburbs

### Key Findings

**Distribution**:
- Range: 2.3% to 33.0%
- Median: 14.1%
- Top 20%: >18.5% volunteer rate

**Correlations** (Pearson):
- Median Income: **r = +0.230** (weak positive)
- Value Score: **r = +0.192** (weak positive)
- Quality Index: **r = +0.178** (weak positive)
- Tertiary Education %: **r = +0.175** (weak positive)

**Interpretation**:
- Volunteer rate has **weak but consistent positive** correlation with quality metrics
- Higher-income suburbs volunteer slightly more
- NOT a strong predictor, but a **tie-breaker** signal
- Suggests social capital complements economic capital

2. **Comparing rural vs urban**:
   - Rural has systematically higher volunteer rates

3. **For apartments/high-density**:
   - Apartments have lower volunteer rates by design
   - Doesn't mean bad investment

## Limitations & Caveats

### Neighbor Arbitrage Limitations

1. **Manual Region Assignment**: Regions defined by name patterns, not official boundaries
   - Risk: Misclassified suburbs
   - Mitigation: Visit in person, verify shared amenities

2. **Regional Averages Include "Other Sydney"**: 1,230 suburbs in "Other" category
   - Risk: Not truly comparable
   - Mitigation: Focus on 7 major defined regions

3. **Prestige Premium Ignored**: Value model doesn't account for "exclusivity"
   - Risk: Missing intangible appeal
   - Mitigation: If you care about prestige, pay the premium consciously

4. **Transport Access Assumed**: Not all suburbs in region have same train access
   - Risk: Some "arbitrage" suburbs are car-dependent
   - Example: Dural vs Turramurra (both Upper North Shore, different transport)

### Volunteer Rate Limitations

1. **Weak Correlations**: r = 0.15-0.23 means only 2-5% of variance explained
   - Implication: Not a strong predictor, just a weak signal

2. **Rural Bias**: Small towns have 25-33% rates, metro has 10-15%
   - Not comparable across contexts

3. **Age Effect**: Retirees volunteer more
   - High volunteer might mean aging community, not necessarily engaged families

4. **Census 2021 Snapshot**: COVID-19 may have affected volunteering
   - Rates could normalize post-pandemic
