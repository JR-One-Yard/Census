## Executive Summary

This comprehensive analysis examines the housing affordability crisis across Australia using the 2021 Census data, combining income quintiles, housing tenure types, mortgage/rent stress indicators, dwelling characteristics, family composition, and age demographics to identify:

**Data Coverage**: 7,922 suburbs with sufficient population (>100 people, >50 dwellings)

## Part 6: Methodology

### 6.1 Data Sources

**Census Tables Used:**
- **G02**: Medians and averages (income, rent, mortgage, age)
- **G37**: Tenure type by dwelling structure
- **G38**: Mortgage repayments by dwelling type
- **G39**: Mortgage repayments by family composition
- **G40**: Rent by landlord type
- **G41**: Number of bedrooms by dwelling structure
- **G33**: Household income by household composition
- **G32**: Family income by family composition
- **G01**: Demographics (age, sex, education)

**Geographic Level**: SAL (Suburbs and Localities) - 15,352 total suburbs

---

### 6.2 Stress Ratio Calculations

**Mortgage Stress Ratio:**
```
Mortgage Stress Ratio = (Monthly Mortgage Payment / Monthly Household Income) √ó 100

Where:
- Monthly Mortgage Payment = Median_mortgage_repay_monthly (from G02)
- Monthly Household Income = (Median_tot_hhd_inc_weekly √ó 52) / 12

Thresholds:
- <30%: Manageable
- 30-40%: Moderate stress
- 40-50%: Severe stress
- >50%: Extreme stress
```

**Rent Stress Ratio:**
```
Rent Stress Ratio = (Weekly Rent / Weekly Household Income) √ó 100

Where:
- Weekly Rent = Median_rent_weekly (from G02)
- Weekly Household Income = Median_tot_hhd_inc_weekly (from G02)

Thresholds: Same as mortgage stress
```

---

### 6.3 Composite Scores

**Homeownership Lockout Score:**
```
Lockout Score = (% Renting √ó 0.4) +
                (Rent Stress Ratio √ó 0.3) +
                (% Young Adults √ó 0.2) +
                ((100 - % Owned Outright) √ó 0.1)

Higher score = Greater lockout
```

**Family Lockout Score:**
```
Family Lockout Score = (% Small Dwellings √ó 0.3) +
                       (Rent Stress Ratio √ó 0.3) +
                       (% Low Income √ó 0.2) +
                       (Persons per Bedroom √ó 10 √ó 0.2)

Higher score = Greater lockout
```

**Crisis Score:**
```
Crisis Score = (Mortgage Stress Ratio √ó 0.25) +
               (Rent Stress Ratio √ó 0.25) +
               (% Renting √ó 0.2) +
               (% Low Income √ó 0.15) +
               (Persons per Bedroom √ó 10 √ó 0.15)

Higher score = Higher crisis risk
```

**Affordability Score:**
```
Affordability Score = ((100 - Mortgage Stress Ratio) √ó 0.3) +
                      ((100 - Rent Stress Ratio) √ó 0.3) +
                      (% Houses √ó 0.2) +
                      ((100 - % Small Dwellings) √ó 0.2)

Higher score = Better affordability
```

## Conclusion

This analysis reveals a stark housing affordability crisis affecting specific demographics and geographic areas in Australia:

**Most Vulnerable:**
1. Young adults (15-34) locked out of homeownership
2. Families in inadequate, overcrowded housing
3. Low-income households (bottom 42%) facing extreme rent stress

**Crisis Epicenters:**
- 805 suburbs in severe crisis (top 10%)
- Characterized by extreme stress ratios (often >50%)
- Low incomes mismatched with high housing costs
- High rental dependency with limited pathways to ownership

**Opportunities:**
- 4,345 sweet spot suburbs offer affordability
- Low stress ratios, manageable costs, stable communities
- Viable alternatives for homebuyers and renters

**Critical Need:**
Immediate intervention in crisis areas combined with long-term structural reforms to:
- Support young adults into homeownership
- Provide adequate family housing
- Align incomes with housing costs
- Prevent further deterioration in vulnerable areas

**Next Steps:**
1. Target relief to 805 identified crisis suburbs
2. Promote sweet spot areas for first home buyers
3. Implement early warning monitoring system
4. Regular census data analysis to track trends
