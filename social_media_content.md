## Reddit Post (r/AusFinance, r/Sydney, r/Australia)

```
TITLE: [OC] I analyzed the 2021 Census data to answer: Should millennial families leave Australia? Here's what I found.

POST:

I'm a data analyst and a millennial parent in Sydney. Like many of you, I've wondered if we can actually afford to stay here long-term.

Instead of doomscrolling property listings at 2am, I decided to analyze the 2021 Australian Census data properly. Here's what 25.4 million data points tell us.

## THE HARD NUMBERS

**Aging Population:**
- Current dependency ratio: 26.7 retirees per 100 workers
- Projected 2040: 40-45 per 100 workers
- That's a 70% increase in fiscal burden
- If you're 35 now, you'll be 50 when this hits hardest

**Sydney Housing:**
- Median price-to-income ratio: 10-13x
- International standard for "severely unaffordable": 5.1x
- Sydney is literally 2x worse than "severely unaffordable"
- Median family income $110k = $1.1M-1.4M median house

**Income Potential (Ages 35-44):**
- Total cohort: 3.49 million people
- 22.7% earn $104k+/year
- 5.5% earn $182k+/year
- Median: ~$52k-$65k/year

## THE ANALYSIS

I built a decision framework based on three scenarios:

**Scenario 1: Stay in Australia**
- Requires: Top 20-30% income ($104k+ individual, $150k-250k household)
- Strategy: Out-earn the housing crisis
- Risk: Aging population increases taxes significantly by 2040
- Best for: Those who can achieve high income and don't mind higher taxes

**Scenario 2: Leave for Better Opportunity**
- Targets: USA (higher salaries), UAE (tax-free), Singapore (Asia gateway)
- Strategy: Geographic arbitrage - earn more, pay less tax
- Risk: Leaving family/network, cultural adjustment
- Best for: Career-focused, willing to relocate for 30-50% income increase

**Scenario 3: Leave for Affordability**
- Targets: Regional Australia first, then Portugal/Spain, Southeast Asia
- Strategy: Lower cost of living, same or remote income
- Risk: Less opportunity, lifestyle changes
- Best for: Those who can work remotely or have portable income

## MY RECOMMENDATION: The 5-Year Test

Don't make an emotional decision. Use data.

**Target metrics by age 40:**
- Net worth: $300k-500k
- Household income: $150k-250k
- Housing: Clear path to ownership OR strategic renting + investing

**If you hit these targets:** Staying in Australia is viable. You've built sufficient wealth to buffer against future tax increases.

**If you don't:** Seriously evaluate relocation. You're 40, kids are ~10-12, still young enough to adapt.

**After age 45:** Window closes (kids enter high school, much harder to move).

## THE 3-PHASE STRATEGY

**Phase 1 (Ages 35-37): Employment**
- Get highest-paying job possible ($100k-150k target)
- Save 30-40% of after-tax income
- Build skills and capital
- Goal: $100k-150k saved in 2-3 years

**Phase 2 (Ages 37-40): Side Hustle**
- Start business while keeping job
- Validate concept with real customers
- Use employment income for stability
- Goal: $30k-50k/year from business

**Phase 3 (Ages 40+): Scale**
- Full-time business OR senior employment
- $150k-300k+ income target
- Reinvest in wealth building
- Goal: $500k-1M net worth by 45
```
